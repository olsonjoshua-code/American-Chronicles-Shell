Unit 8: Modern America

Use these briefing slides to study the major ideas, events, and turning points of Unit 8. This unit follows the conservative turn under Reagan, the end of the Cold War, the rise of red-state and blue-state politics, the challenges of globalization, and the modern debate over America's founding ideals.

Overview

What this unit is about

This unit explores how the United States redefined politics, power, and national purpose from the 1980s into the early twenty-first century. It moves from Reagan's conservative revolution to the collapse of the Cold War, then into polarization, globalization, terrorism, and renewed arguments over liberty, equality, security, and government.

Study Guide

Unit Focus

How did the United States redefine politics, power, and national purpose from the conservative turn of the 1980s through the debates of the early twenty-first century?

What to Notice

  • How Reagan's rise shifted debates over taxes, regulation, labor, and the size of government.
  • How the Cold War ended through pressure, reform, protest, and Soviet weakness rather than one single cause.
  • How polarization, close elections, and globalization reshaped politics after the Cold War.
  • How modern arguments over security, equality, opportunity, and federal power connect back to founding ideals.

Study Tip

Track the arc across the whole unit: conservative realignment, the end of the Cold War, domestic polarization, global-age foreign policy, and then the final civic debate over what American ideals should mean in modern life.
