    Section Roadmap

    Election Debate Johnson and Goldwater clashed over whether bigger government meant progress or danger.
    Great Society Congress passed sweeping reforms on poverty, rights, health care, education, immigration, and culture.
    Warren Court The Supreme Court expanded voting equality, church-state limits, and protections for the accused.

    What to Watch For

    1. How the 1964 election became a debate over liberty versus activist government.
    2. Why Johnson's experience in Congress mattered so much for passing reforms.
    3. Which programs attacked poverty, expanded health care, and widened opportunity.
    4. How the Warren Court changed everyday rights in politics, schools, and criminal justice.

    Study Tip

    Break the chapter into three buckets: the 1964 election, Johnson's reform programs, and Warren Court rulings. Once you sort details into those three categories, the lesson becomes much easier to organize.
