What this unit explores

Unit 7A follows the rise and decline of 1960s optimism. Students move from the image of Camelot and Kennedy’s promise, through reform and the Great Society, into counterculture, conflict, and America’s growing involvement in Vietnam.

Unit Focus

How did the optimism of the early 1960s give way to conflict, reform, protest, and deeper national division?

What to Notice

  1. How public image and political reality were often different.
  2. How Cold War fears shaped both domestic and foreign policy.
  3. How reform movements pushed the country to change.
  4. How hope, conflict, and protest all defined the 1960s.

Study Tip

Do not treat these lessons as separate stories. Try to see the arc: Camelot, reform, backlash, protest, and the expanding crisis in Vietnam all connect to one broader transformation in American life.
